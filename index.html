Player 1

Note

In Room 2 hidden in the news papers and scattered cards are ciphers showing the equivalents of the special characers. % = I, $ = A, @ = E, " = U, and * = O.

Clock

In Room 2 when the briefcase is unlocked a hint appears reading "FOUR TEN, FOUR TEN, FOUR TEN", The number of times four ten appears is a hint. If you psition the Clock at 12:30, the sum of all three of the 4:10s the clue for the next puzzle appears.

Cassete Player

When the radio knobs are turned in the right ways in Room 2 a series of sounds can be heard, player 2 can replay the sounds by clicking the replay button on the top right of the radio's control area. The correct sequence of buttons to press on the cassete player from left to right is, 2nd, 2nd, 6th, 1st, and 5th.

Globe

After player 2 solves the frame puzzle, a photo of the correct orientation of the globe appears, Note that the globe is viewed from the top, to properly orient the globe have North America face the right, and Eurasia Face the left, make sure that Greenland is touching the globes frame.

Pad

After Player 2 solves the frame puzzle a hidden text on the top right inside the frame reads "***492", And when Player 1 solves the globe puzzle a black light highlights the invisible text on the globe that reads "873***". When put together 873492 is the sequence of numbers needed to escape the room.

Player 2

Briefcase

In Room 1 the Note deciphered reads "Third is the sum of its former and follower, it is rather moldy. The first holds nothing, a void so vast. The fifth and sixth burn fiery. To conclude the set, have something citrusy." These all refer to the locks on the briefcase numbered from left to right. The very first lock color is black "The first holds nothing, a void so vast." The second, third and fourth is blue, green, and yellow. "Third is the sum of its former and follower, it is rather moldy." The third being moldy is a hint that it is green, and it being the sum of its former (the lock before it) and follower (the lock in ahead of it) means that blue and yellow are the colors around it. the fifth and fourth are red and orange respectively, "The fifth and sixth burn fiery." Fire is often red and orange, the hint for the last lock being a different color than the fifth is "To conclude the set, have something citrusy." An orange is citrusy.

Radio

In Room 1 after the clock opens its secret, There are four knobs turned in the exact positions the radio knobs need to be in. From left to right position thee red mark at the bottom left, top left, top middle, and far left.

Frame

After playing the correct sequence of sounds on the cassette player, Player 1 should see a polaroid photo of the solution to the frame puzzle is revealed. Top left has the arrow facing left, Top middle is facing down, and top right is facing up. The middle left arrow is facing down, middle middle is facing left, middle right is facing right. The bottom left arrow is facing top left, the bottom middle is facing top right, and the bottom right arrow is facing top left.

How to play

Have two devices and a friend to play the game with you, make sure you and your friend can communicate with each other wheather you're both in the same room, or long distance on call. Then, each of you will have to choose which player they want to be. You cannot both be player 1, or both player 2. There can only be one player 1, and one player 2. Once youre ready click the player buttons to start the game, Remember you CAN NOT show your screen to your friend, you can however describe what you're seing. The key to solving the puzzles is communication, so describe items and symbols as best you can. To make this even more challenging consider setting a timer for one hour, and try to beat all the puzzles before the timer goes off. The game ends once both players have escaped their respective rooms.
